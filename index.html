🔴 Phase 3 — Central Sensitization
The spinal cord and brain are rewired. Pain amplified everywhere. Even after tissue heals, the nervous system stays in alarm mode.
🧠 Cortical Smudging — The Brain Changes Shape
Chronic pain and dysfunction literally reorganize the somatosensory cortex — the area of your brain that maps body parts. Affected regions become distorted and overlap with neighboring areas. Patients experience this as diffuse, hard-to-locate pain that seems to "spread." Brain imaging studies confirm this is measurable structural change — and chiropractic care has been shown to begin reversing it.
fMRI Documented
Hyperalgesia: Volume Turned Up
Central sensitization amplifies pain signals at the spinal cord level. Normal sensations become painful. Mild stimuli cause disproportionate responses. The nervous system is no longer accurately representing reality — it's in a persistent state of alarm.
Allodynia: Pain Without Cause
In a sensitized nervous system, non-painful inputs — light touch, temperature changes, normal movement — trigger pain responses. This is why some patients hurt from the lightest contact or even clothing pressure.
Why This Justifies Comprehensive Care: Treating only the symptom is futile when the problem is now in the nervous system itself. Correction requires consistent, properly dosed neurological input over time to reverse the sensitization pattern — exactly what a structured chiropractic care plan provides.
